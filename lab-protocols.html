Protocol 01: Cryopreservation and Recovery Protocol for Caenorhabditis elegans

Cryopreservation is an essential method for maintaining C. elegans strains over long periods. Freezing nematodes properly ensures their viability upon thawing, allowing for reliable revival and continued research.

Materials Required

  • C. elegans cultures
  • Nematode Growth Medium (NGM) plates seeded with E. coli OP50
  • M9 buffer (Composition: NaCl, KH₂PO₄, Na₂HPO₄, H₂O)
  • Freezing solution: Soft Agar Freezing Solution
  • Cryovials (1.5–2 ml)
  • Ice bucket
  • Sterilized metal spatula
  • -80°C freezer or liquid nitrogen storage

Step-by-Step Protocol

  1. Culturing Worms: Grow C. elegans on NGM plates with E. coli OP50. Incubate at 20°C until worms reach the just-starved L1-L2 stage.
  2. Harvesting Worms: Wash each plate with 1.5 ml of M9 buffer to collect worms. Transfer suspensions to a 15 ml conical tube.
  3. Cooling and Mixing: Place the tube on ice for 15 minutes. Add an equal volume (3 ml) of the pre-warmed Soft Agar Freezing Solution.
  4. Aliquoting and Freezing: Transfer 1.5 ml of the mixture into labeled cryovials. Store vials in a styrofoam box at -80°C for slow cooling.

Recovery Procedure

  1. Thawing: Retrieve a cryovial from storage. Thaw rapidly at room temperature.
  2. Plating and Incubation: Transfer thawed suspension onto a seeded NGM plate. Incubate at 20°C for 48–72 hours.

Key Considerations

  • Use of Glycerol: Reduces ice crystal formation, preventing cellular damage during freezing.
  • Cooling Rate: A slow cooling rate is essential for worm viability; rapid freezing results in poor survival.
  • Optimal Worm Stage: L1-L2 stage worms have the highest post-thaw survival rate.
  • Labeling: Always label cryovials with strain information and date.

References

  • Protocols.io (2020). Freezing worms protocol.
  • Hayden, J., et al. (2021). Cryopreservation of dehydrated Caenorhabditis elegans.

Protocol 02: Bleaching C. elegans to Obtain Eggs and L1s

Bleach solution will kill worms but will not destroy eggs because they contain a protective eggshell.

CAUTION:

While handling bleach, wear gloves, goggles, and a lab coat.

Materials

  • Bleach
  • Sodium hydroxide (NaOH)
  • 1× M9 buffer (Dilute 10× M9 1:10 before using; sterile)
  • Adult gravid hermaphrodites
  • 100-mm seeded plates for recovery

Step-by-Step Protocol

  1. Prepare Fresh Bleach/NaOH Solution: (Do not use if older than 1 week.)
  2. Harvest Gravid Worms: Wash gravid worms from plates with 1× M9 buffer into a 15-ml conical tube.
  3. Bleaching Treatment: Add 3 ml of bleach/NaOH mix and monitor adult lysis under a microscope.
  4. Egg Recovery: Immediately spin for no more than 1 min at 3000 rpm.

References

  • Porta-de-la-Riva M, et al. Basic Caenorhabditis elegans Methods: Synchronization and Observation.

Protocol 03: Generating Males by Heat Shock

Males arise spontaneously from hermaphrodite self-fertilization at about 1 in 500 eggs laid.

Materials

  • Six or more plates with at least five L4 hermaphrodites of the desired genotype on each
  • Incubator at 30°C

Step-by-Step Protocol

  1. Incubate the plates: with the L4 hermaphrodites for 4 to 6 hours at 30°C.
  2. Return the plates: to 20°C. After 3 days, screen the plates for males.

References
